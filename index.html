Frequently Asked Questions

I want to start the practice of decluttering. Where do I begin?

Begin where the energy is. Don't start in the most dreaded, difficult spot. Start with the room that, if cleared, would bring you the most joy or peace. The practice is fueled by small wins. Start there, and let the momentum build.

How do I practice good 'dumpster etiquette' in my neighborhood?

The practice of being a good neighbor is key. Let your immediate neighbors know a dumpster will be arriving. Load it thoughtfully, ensuring no debris is hanging over the sides. And if you finish early, call us for pickup. It's about respecting the shared space.

What is the practice for loading heavy materials like concrete?

The practice here is balance. Load heavy items first and distribute them evenly across the bottom of the roll off container. Do not pile them all up in one spot. This makes the load safer and easier to transport. For large amounts, ask about a dedicated concrete dumpster.

I'm a contractor. What is your practice for rush jobs or swaps?

We honor the urgency of your practice. While we can’t control traffic, we prioritize swaps for our professional clients. Call us as soon as you know a dumpster is getting full, and we will get a swap scheduled into our routes as quickly as possible to minimize your crew’s downtime.

Do you have a practice for protecting my driveway?

We do. The practice of care. Our drivers are trained to set down the dumpsters gently. Upon request, we will always place wooden planks under the dumpster’s contact points to protect your property. Just ask.
